Machine Learning, Data, and Decision Systems

I work at the intersection of machine learning, data, and decision systems, studying how modeling choices and learned representations behave under realistic data conditions and evaluation constraints.

Background

Training

My background spans applied machine learning, data science, and quantitative analysis, with formal training in business, statistics, and modern ML methods.

I have completed applied data science training through MIT Professional Education and continue to build a research-oriented portfolio focused on representation behavior, robustness, and evaluation.

Research

Interests

My research interests include representation learning, robustness under distribution shift, data imbalance, and evaluation practices for machine learning systems deployed in real-world decision contexts.
